Mision

We are a dedicated company that connects farmers with consumers, selling organic products through our web and mobile application, where we offer a wide variety of organic foods grown in partnership with farmers linked to the Corporación Autónoma Regional del Cauca (C.R.C), we seek to generate environmental awareness and generate good habits of life for our followers

Vision

Colcart will be the biggest enterprise dedicated to opening an educational and commercial window of organic based products for the farmers and consumers in the department of Cauca, Colombia.

Our team

We are all Colombian students from the University of Cauca and have complemented our project with students of different disciplines with the end of boosting integrity, inclusion and more creativity in our project. Thus, we count with a greater amount of wisdom, experience in different topics such as entrepreneurship, leadership, programming, mathematics, software design, Internet of Things (IoT), polyglot language skills, business management wisdom and excellent public communication skills, among others. In this way we have created a multitalented and strong team.

Why

We would like to highlight that our aim is to generate a social impact by boosting the farms social inclusion in the department of Cauca, because we consider they must be paid what they deserve and we think we can empower them to become healthy products entrepreneurs of their own farms. This will undoubtedly have a very positive social impact on the quality of life of this vulnerable population, which will have an impact on better health conditions as we as on ourselves, the general population who daily consume their products.

Problem Statement

75% of farmers in Colombia earn under the minimum wage, generating a low-life quality standard, particularly in rural areas, leading to a migratory process of these farmers towards the city and creating a decrease in job opportunities and more unemployed holders. It is also highlighted that 40% of the monthly family shopping basket comes from farmers. Secondly, there are high manufacturing costs for producing organic products. Moreover, people are not usually willing to pay for them at a fair price. Finally, from the 21 million potential lands for viable organic production, 1) only 0.25% of it (54,000 hectares) are effectively agricultural dedicated and chemical free . Since nowadays there's an abundance of agrochemicals such as pesticides, fertilizers, and biochemicals for pests control, which lead to the pollution of water-supply sources and natural ecosystems that feed most of the food supply chain that we usually buy through intermediaries.
